Introduction

Lal Suhanra National Park is situated near Bahawalpur in the Punjab province of Pakistan. Established in 1972, it is one of the largest national parks in South Asia and is recognized as a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve. The park is unique for its diverse landscape, which includes desert, forest, and wetland ecosystems, making it a vital sanctuary for a wide variety of flora and fauna.

Location Bahawalpur District, Punjab.
Area 162,568 acres (approx.)
Established 1972
Key Feature Blackbuck Breeding & Patisar Lake

Landscapes & Ecosystems

The park offers a fascinating blend of three distinct ecosystems: the desert land of the Cholistan Desert, irrigated forest plantations, and the expansive wetland of Patisar Lake. This diversity allows the park to support species adapted to arid environments as well as those dependent on water bodies and dense vegetation.

Biodiversity And Wildlife

Lal Suhanra is famous for its population of Blackbuck, which are bred here in captivity for reintroduction. The park is also home to Chinkara Gazelle, Nilgai (Blue Bull), and Hog Deer. The wetlands attract numerous migratory birds and waterfowl, while the desert areas support reptiles and desert-adapted mammals.

Mammals Blackbuck, Chinkara, Nilgai, Hog Deer, Indian Rhinoceros (in enclosure), and Wild Boar.
Birds Houbara Bustard, Griffon Vulture, Honey Buzzard, and various waterfowl species at Patisar Lake.
Attractions Lion Safari, Rhino Enclosure, and Blackbuck Breeding Center.

Conservation Efforts

The park plays a crucial role in the conservation of the Blackbuck antelope. A dedicated breeding center has been established to increase their numbers. Additionally, the park management focuses on preserving the wetland ecosystem of Patisar Lake and protecting the indigenous flora of the Cholistan desert from encroachment and overgrazing.
